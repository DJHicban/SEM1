Ateneo Secured their Final Four slot against ADU

Ateneo de Manila University restored its end-game pride on Saturday, grounding the low-flying Adamson University to secure a Final Four comeback in the 77th UAAP men’s basketball tournament at the Mall of Asia Arena.

Playing with more resolve and urgency, the  Eagles shot down the winless Falcons, 67-52, to book their tenth victory and the all-important Final Four spot after missing the post-season last year.

But the Eagles, who ducked a last-second scare from University of the Philippines in their previous game, underscored their recovery with the greater task that lies ahead of them.

“Masaya at satisfied din but we agreed that we’re going to go all the way this year. That’s going to be a tremendous task for us but cliché as it may sound but if we really believe it I know we can do this,” Ateneo coach Bo Perasol said.

“We’re still aiming for that Top 2 spots.”

With the win, the Eagles now climbed to 10-3, just half a game behind league-leader Far Eastern University (10-2), and gained more inside track for a twice-to-beat advantage heading into the Final Four wars.

Nursing just a 23-19 edge, Ateneo opened the second quarter with an 8-0 run to make it an 11-point lead.

Yet 

the Falcons

 quickly responded with their own 8-0 blitz capped by threes from Don Trollano and Christian Garcia to cut the deficit, 27-34. The Eagles then dropped a 10-4 bomb to re-establish a double-digit lead, 41-31, at the half.

 

Despite leading, the Eagles were not yet out of the woods as Adamson came within six on a Joseph Nalos fastbreak lay-up and Trollano’s freethrows early in the third.

However, Ateneo got a lucky break when the entire Adamson bench was slapped with a technical foul for improper decorum after contesting a Don Trollano whistle at the 9:01 mark of the period.

The call broke Adamson’s momentum as the Falcons went scoreless in the last 5:10 minutes of the game while the Eagles unleashed a 15-0 onslaught to erect their biggest lead at 56-35.

“I really feel that our energy was waning. Thankfully our defense kicked in in the third and early fourth quarter,” said Perasol, noting the Eagles’ tough three-game schedule in seven days.

Kiefer Ravena led the Eagles with 16 points while Chris Newsome added 11.

Trollano meanwhile scored 20 to pace Adamsdon, which dropped to 0-13.
